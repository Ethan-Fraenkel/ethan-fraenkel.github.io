Projects

  • “Risen From The Ruins: The Politics And Culture Of Jewish Anti-Fascism In The German Democratic Republic (1949-1989)": My undergraduate Honors thesis in History which examines the relationship between German-Jewish artists, politicians, and the anti-fascist narrative of the East German state during the Cold War. I received the John W. Wilkes European History Award from NYU for the thesis as well as funding through the College of Arts and Science Dean's Undergraduate Research Fund.

  • "Kybernetisch": An interactive zine about the relationship between cybernetics and literature co-created with a classmate for the NYU course "Translating Knowledge Alphabets Across Media." The zine incorporates analytic discourse, thought experiments, and coding to explore the implications of computer-generated, stochastic language.

  • “Who will I be tomorrow under Columbus’ Atlantic banners? Some Portraits of Arab(-American) diaspora": An original photo-journalistic piece exploring the Arab American diaspora for "Al Andalus in New York", Molly Crabapple’s zine highlighting the historical traces of Andalusian Spain that remain in contemporary New York.
