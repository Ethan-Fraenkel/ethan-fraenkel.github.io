About Me

I grew up in Cambridge and subsequently Newton, Massachusetts. I spent most of my college years in NYC, where I graduated from NYU with a B.A. in History and Comparative Literature.

Recently, I've worked as a freelance translator and research assistant engaging with archival materials in Yiddish and German.

Owing to my passion for listening to music across many genres, I hosted two weekly internet radio shows for WNYU dedicated to new alternative music and Jewish music in diasporic languages, respectively. My other hobbies include reading literature from across the globe, language learning, bouldering, skateboarding, and baking.
